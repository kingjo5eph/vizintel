Property Taxes by State and County, 2024

  • Overview
  • Key Takeaways
  • Data Sources

The "Property Taxes by State and County 2024" analysis shows significant regional variation in property taxes, with some counties experiencing increases of up to 17.8% between 2021 and 2022. Overall, rising property values and tax rate adjustments have driven steady growth in property tax revenues across many states. Notable disparities exist, with certain regions, particularly economically growing areas, seeing higher tax increases. These trends underscore the ongoing importance of property taxes in supporting state and local finances.

  • Property taxes have generally increased from 2021 to 2022 across most counties.
  • There are significant variations in tax growth rates between counties, highlighting local economic and policy factors.
  • Inflation-adjusted changes provide a clearer picture of real tax growth beyond nominal increases, offering insights into the real burden on homeowners.

Electric Power Utilization sales and Revenue

  • Overview
  • Key Takeaways
  • Data Sources

The dataset analyzes electric power utilization, sales, and revenue across U.S. utility companies. Residential services dominate revenue, with commercial and industrial sectors contributing significantly despite smaller customer bases. Regional differences are driven by climate and industrial activity, while ownership models, such as investor-owned and municipal utilities, influence service focus and investment.

  • Residential services are the largest revenue generators.
  • Industrial sectors consume the most electricity per customer.
  • Regional and climate factors shape electricity demand.
  • Ownership models influence utilities' service strategies and investment decisions.
